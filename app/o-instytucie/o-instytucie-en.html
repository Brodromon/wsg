European Institute

European Institute of Nicolaus Copernicus is an inter-faculty organizational unit based in Bydgoszcz. The basic tasks of the European Institute include:
  • supervision over the activities of all organizational units of the University in Ukraine. Correlation with activities throughout Eastern Europe and the Middle East, in particular with regard to the educational or research offer based on the Russian language
  • coordination of cooperation on the basis of partnership relations between Ukrainian institutions and WSG units
  • initiating and developing organizational structures responsible for cooperation with Ukrainian partners and partners from other Eastern European countries
  • conducting scientific research in the fields and disciplines with which the fields of study run by the University's basic units and in the field of scientific creativity, including the publication of scientific works, are related
  • coordination of works carried out within projects through thematic networks or by consortia in which the University will take part

As part of the European Institute, there will be four centers: the Center for Preparatory Courses (CPC) - the aim of which is to prepare candidates for studies coming from Ukraine to undertake studies conducted by WSG basic units operating in Poland. In this connection, the so-called basic courses that will deepen the basic knowledge and skills in the field of the planned field of study and the so-called preparatory courses, which will focus on improving the degree of adaptation to take up and continue studies in Poland.

The next unit is the Center of Culture and Polish Language (CCPL), which will run language courses, preparing to study in Polish. The CCPL unit will be the basis for the creation of the Center of Culture and Languages of Central Europe, in which teaching of foreign languages will be taught - mainly Slavic languages in which students want to study abroad, especially in EU countries. In addition to teaching Polish to foreigners, CCPL will conduct Polish language certification - including certification for teachers - as well as provide accreditation for language schools operating in Ukraine. The task of CCPL will also be to create a program of educational and cultural cooperation with cultural institutions and cooperation with schools - especially those that operate among the Polish minority or those teaching Polish.

The third organizational unit, operating within the structures of the European Institute, is the Academic Cooperation Center (ACC) - responsible for extending, intensifying, coordinating and deepening the cooperation of WSG with Ukrainian universities. The most important tasks of the unit will include: implementation of double diploma programs, accreditation of education programs of other Ukrainian universities, joint educational programs, including post-graduate studies, trainings for academic staff and internships, as well as internships for students and cooperation in the field of development and implementation projects aimed at improving the quality of education, quality of study, program standards, methods of verifying students' competences, as well as administrative service of nostrification of academic degrees of the staff of potential academic teachers who gained degrees in Ukraine (not subject to recognition of their equivalence with degrees acquired in Poland under contracts) international).

The fourth unit within the Institute is the Entrepreneurship Support Center (ESC), which will be responsible for the cooperation of WSG - including the Institute itself - with business partners from Ukraine. The university has well-organized units dedicated to establishing and conducting cooperation with enterprises and business environment institutions at the headquarters of the University. A model for establishing contacts between universities and business, linking academic and business partners and identifying entrepreneurs' needs was developed. This model is intended to be applied to Ukrainian enterprises. In addition, it is also about activities aimed at incubating entrepreneurship, conducting training for entrepreneurs and company representatives, developing entrepreneurial attitudes among the general public, including Ukrainian school and university youth.

Nicolaus Copernicus as the Patron of the Institute of the European University of Economics

The University authorities, in consultation with foreign partners, have decided to give the European Institute the name of Nicolaus Copernicus. The patron symbolizes the objectives of the Institute of the European WSG, and at the same time being universally recognized throughout the world, including in Eastern Europe and the Middle East, does not raise any controversy about a cultural, philosophical or religious nature, despite the fact that he was a priest and fulfilled important church's functions.

The significance of the Copernicus discovery, often referred to as the "Copernican revolution", puts it in the row of the most prominent figures of science in the history of the world, among such giants of mind as: Kepler, Newton, Darwin, Einstein, Hubble, Fleming or Heisenberg. The choice of Copernicus as a patron also emphasizes the universal values and common heritage connecting Europe and its immediate surroundings, which grew on the ruins of the western and eastern empires, the legacy of Rome and Byzantium. The choice of the patron was also dictated by the recognition of the universal values of humanism and European philosophical thought, which in the inquiries over the nature and cause of the universe, perceived the most perfect manifestation of intelligent life.

Copernicus was one of the best and most distinguished representatives of this thought. One can successfully say that he was a man of the Renaissance, universally educated: astronomer, mathematician, lawyer and physician. This outstanding Pole, apart from the heliocentric conception of building the world, also contributed to economic sciences with his treaty on coin minting and taxes.

The choice of the patron was important for his biography, including the history of his family, which begins in Silesia, which was then a borderland, then under the influence of many cultures. Born in Toruń, which remained a model of peaceful and consistent coexistence of people of different cultures and religions. He studied in various cities and countries, in Krakow, Padua and Bologna. He conducted his scientific investigations in Warmia, in which these influences were clearly marked. He was, therefore, a thoroughly universalist character - how characteristic of the role of the Republic at the turn of the 15th and 16th centuries as an intermediary between the West and the East of Europe. It was able to absorb the technical solutions of the Ottoman Empire which was pressing south of Europe, which also mediated in the cultural influences of peoples and nations scattered on the southern and eastern shores of the Mediterranean.

Also the times of the breakthrough in which he lived, favored taking up new challenges, making bold hypotheses, providing new justifications, in short, favored bold and unscientific thinking. Copernicus, drawing from the spiritual resources of a bygone era, was able to direct his intellectual potential towards new times, the most important manifestation of which was to become science. Therefore, this modest and unpretentious father of modern science - our compatriot, and at the same time a compatriot from Kujawy and Pomerania - can not become the Patron of the Institution, which formulates such a broad and deep mission.
